Summary Characters Timeline Trivia Filming Author Info

Late 19th Century: Albus Dumbledore, Gellert Grindelwald, and Tom Riddle (Lord Voldemort) are born.

1926: Dumbledore defeats Grindelwald, who later becomes one of the most powerful Dark Wizards in history.

1928-1938: Tom Riddle attends Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry and opens the Chamber of Secrets, unleashing the Basilisk on the school. He frames Hagrid for the crimes and acquires objects known as Horcruxes to attain immortality.

1945: Dumbledore defeats Grindelwald in a legendary duel, and Grindelwald is imprisoned in Nurmengard.

1960s-1970s: The First Wizarding War takes place. Voldemort gains followers, known as Death Eaters, and seeks to establish a pure-blood wizarding society. The Order of the Phoenix, led by Dumbledore, opposes him.

1979-1981: Harry Potter's parents, James and Lily Potter, are murdered by Lord Voldemort, but when he tries to kill their infant son Harry, the curse backfires. Voldemort loses his physical form, and Harry survives with a lightning-shaped scar on his forehead.

1981-1991: Harry is raised by his aunt and uncle, the Dursleys, unaware of his magical heritage. Meanwhile, the wizarding world rebuilds after the war.

1991: Rubeus Hagrid, a giant of a man, delivers Harry's letter of acceptance to Hogwarts. Harry learns about his identity as a wizard and his place in the magical world.

1991-1997: Harry attends Hogwarts, makes friends, and discovers more about the dark events surrounding his past. The rise of Voldemort and the return of dark forces become central to the plot.
